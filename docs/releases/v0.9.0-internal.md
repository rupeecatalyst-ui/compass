# Catalyst One — Internal Release Notes

## Version v0.9.0-internal

**Internal Operational Dry Run · Official Release Record**

---

## 1. Release Overview

Catalyst One **v0.9.0-internal** marks the platform’s first **Internal Operational Dry Run**.

This release consolidates the Loan Origination and Business Operating Platform into a single, build-verified enterprise surface suitable for **real organizational usage** by Rupee Catalyst teams. It is an **Internal Operational Preview**: operators may exercise end-to-end journeys, navigation, and administrative consoles under controlled conditions.

This is **not** a customer-facing production launch. The purpose of this release is to:

- Establish a stable baseline for day-to-day internal operations
- Validate that major modules load, navigate, and compose as one product
- Create an official release record against which feedback and stabilization work can be tracked

Stabilization Phase criteria for this cut have been met: TypeScript compilation, lint, and production build succeeded; deployment preparation for GitHub and Vercel is documented.

## 2. Major Modules Available

The following modules are available in this internal build. Summaries describe product intent and surface readiness—not implementation depth.

### Authentication

Secure sign-in, session refresh, and route protection for the dashboard and protected workspaces. Supports internal dry-run access with environment-configured secrets.

### Dashboard

Operational home for Catalyst One users—entry point into loans, customers, tasks, and enterprise surfaces.

### Customer Management

Customer records and relationship context for origination and servicing workflows, including list and detail patterns used across the loan journey.

### Opportunity Lifecycle & Opportunity Workspace

Unified opportunity working surface for progressing files through business stages, with dialogue, documents, lender, and related panels composed into one workspace experience.

### Opportunity Compass

Visual health and recommendation layer for opportunity posture (needle / pulse signals) to guide operator attention during dry run.

### Loan Board & Loan Workspace

Pipeline (Loan Board) and loan-file workspaces for stage-oriented origination work, including operational boards and file-centric views.

### Customer 360 / Customer Context

Customer-centric views that assemble relationship and loan context for operators reviewing a single customer across the platform.

### Workflow Engine (EWOE)

Enterprise Workflow Orchestration Engine surfaces for workflow administration and runtime-oriented workflow views used in opportunity and loan journeys.

### Experience Console (EEI)

Enterprise Experience Intelligence console for decision-oriented and advisory experience views within the operating platform.

### Credit & Risk Engine

Admin console for credit and risk policy, rules, lenders, products, models, simulation, version history, and audit—policy administration for the enterprise credit stack.

### Document Intelligence (EDIE)

Context-aware document rules and simulation-oriented document actions, integrated with operational dialogue logging for dry-run document journeys.

### Partner / Lender Management

Lender and partner-facing surfaces for selection, mapping, and operational lender work (including LIFE lender executor selection where enabled).

### Task Engine

Independent and opportunity-linked tasks with overdue escalation patterns for operational follow-up during the dry run.

### Dialogue Center

Unified operational timeline of stage, task, document, notification, and workflow events for opportunity context.

### Contact Master

Multi-role enterprise contacts with mandatory mobile and email-prompt workflows for incomplete contact profiles.

### Communication (ENCE)

Notification and communication simulation policies and templates. External delivery remains disabled for this internal release.

### Mission Control

Executive command center shell with dedicated operational centers (see below). Accessible from Admin Console navigation for privileged roles.

### Enterprise Search

Unified search center across Catalyst One modules via provider-backed search architecture (placeholder indexing for this release).

### Executive Briefing (CHANAKYA)

Executive landing brief within Mission Control—greeting, brief card, decision workspace hooks, and quick actions for leadership orientation.

### Situation Room

Widget-driven executive awareness surface for operational domain health, alerts, and navigation into related Mission Control centers.

### Alert Center

Centralized enterprise alert surface for severity, category, and module-oriented alert review (provider / placeholder event streams).

### Security Operations

Security Operations Center for domain health, events, threats, identity/session summaries, compliance status, and security quick actions (architecture-first for this release).

### Observability

Enterprise Observability Center for platform/engine health, services, performance, errors, jobs, queues, availability, and dependencies (contract / placeholder collectors).

### Horizon

Strategic Planning Workspace: Initiative → Workstream → Milestone → Activity hierarchy with portfolio overview and initiative workspace tooling.

### Organization

Company profile, directors, corporate repository, bank accounts, digital signatures, and company seal—Super Admin organization master data.

### Enterprise Libraries

Product Library, Enterprise Asset Library, and Architecture (including ATLAS) administration for enterprise registries, lifecycle, and documentation.

### Configuration (ECG & System Modes)

Enterprise Configuration Grants (ECG) and System Modes for workflow, communication, Chanakya, and migration-oriented platform mode controls.

### Settings, Accounting, Reports & AI Assistant

Platform settings and shell routes for accounting, reports, and AI assistant. Treat AI and analytics-backed reports as limited / placeholder for this dry run (see Known Limitations).

## 3. Enterprise Frameworks

Reusable frameworks underpin Mission Control and related enterprise surfaces. They provide shared contracts, registries, and providers so centers remain consistent without embedding domain engines in the UI.

### Enterprise Widget Framework

Layout, shell, and permission metadata for Mission Control widget-driven pages (e.g. Situation Room).

### Enterprise Search Framework

Search entity, publisher, category, and ranking contracts with adapters projecting into the Enterprise Search Center.

### Enterprise Alert Framework

Alert publish, channel, lifecycle, and render contracts consumed by the Alert Center and related executive surfaces.

### Enterprise Security Framework

Security domain, event, threat, session, policy, and compliance contracts projected into Security Operations.

### Enterprise Observability Framework

Health registry, service/telemetry/metrics/queue contracts, and projections for the Observability Center.

### Executive Intelligence Framework

Insight, narrative, and brief model pipelines supporting CHANAKYA Executive Briefing and decision-oriented presentation.

### Enterprise Experience / Decision Stack

Decision engine, knowledge/reasoning, advisory console, and experience intelligence foundations that support the Experience Console and related decision UX (architecture and simulation posture for dry run).

These frameworks exist to **standardize enterprise infrastructure**—not to imply live external integrations in v0.9.0-internal.

## 5. Known Limitations

The following capabilities are intentionally **placeholder, simulated, or deferred** in v0.9.0-internal. They must not be treated as live production services during the dry run.

- **AI** — No live model execution or AI decisioning
- **Telemetry collection** — Observability contracts without production collectors
- **External APIs** — Partner / lender / third-party live integrations not assumed
- **Live alerts** — Alert Center uses provider / placeholder streams
- **Search indexing** — Search Center without production index backends
- **Observability collectors** — Metrics, traces, and queue pressure are architectural / sample
- **Background jobs** — Job and queue panels are non-executing for this cut
- **Analytics & reporting engines** — Reports / accounting depth limited for dry run
- **External communication delivery** — ENCE simulation only; outbound channels disabled
- **Document file storage** — Document actions are simulated / logged, not a full DMS
- **Disabled Mission Control modules** — Registry entries such as Digital Twin and AI Control Tower remain unavailable until explicitly enabled

Operators should report gaps against this list rather than treating them as unexpected defects—unless a listed surface **crashes**, **blocks navigation**, or **misrepresents a claimed live capability**.

## 6. Internal Dry Run Objectives

During the internal operational period, the organization will:

1. **Validate workflows** across opportunity, loan, document, task, and dialogue journeys  
2. **Collect usability feedback** from operators and administrators  
3. **Measure perceived performance** of primary screens and navigation  
4. **Identify bottlenecks** in multi-module journeys and admin consoles  
5. **Verify business processes** against real internal operating procedures  
6. **Validate permissions and role boundaries** (including Super Admin surfaces)  
7. **Improve navigation clarity** between Dashboard, Mission Control, Horizon, and Admin Console  
8. **Improve stability** based on dry-run incidents and recovery behavior  
9. **Confirm deployment and environment practices** (secrets, URLs, smoke checks)  
10. **Prioritize Operational Stabilization** work for the next milestone

## 7. Deferred Roadmap

The following are **intentionally deferred** beyond v0.9.0-internal and are not success criteria for this dry run:

- Full **Enterprise Communication Hub** (live multi-channel delivery and inbox)
- Advanced **Relationship Intelligence** beyond current contact / customer foundations
- Dedicated **Backup & Recovery Center**
- Future **AI capabilities** (assistants, underwriting aids, generative workflows)
- Production **search indexing** and ranking services
- Production **telemetry / APM** pipelines
- Live **security gateway enforcement** beyond architectural SOC surfaces
- Fully enabled **Digital Twin** and **AI Control Tower** Mission Control modules
- Deep **accounting** and **analytics** productization
- Broader customer-facing COMPASS marketing surfaces as part of this Catalyst One cut

Roadmap sequencing remains subject to Operational Stabilization findings.
